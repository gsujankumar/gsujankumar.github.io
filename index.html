About Me

I am currently with Amazon as a Research Scientist.

Prior to joining Amazon, I graduated with a Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign where I was advised by Prof. Naresh Shanbhag. I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology Madras, with a bachelor's and master's degree in Electrical Engineering. My master's thesis was on optical phase-locked loops.

Research Overview

My research broadly spans integrated circuit design and machine learning (ML). Specifically, I am interested in problems that require the implementation of ML on resource-constrained platforms. If you are interested in my research further check out my publications, or this book that I co-authored.

Here are few of my recent projects:

Deep In-Memory Computing Architectures for Machine Learning

This project tackles the problem of high energy and latency costs of data movement between processor and memory. We propose deep in-memory architecture (DIMA) that tackles this problem by embedded computations into the memory arrays. DIMA drastically reduces memory read and data access costs by performing mixed-signal computations in the periphery of the memory array. We estimated that a DIMA-based architecture to accelerate Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) that shows over 5 and 2.4 times simultaneous improvements in energy-efficiency and throughput. We demonstrated the DIMA concept by designed a multi-functional SRAM-based DIMA IC on 65 nm CMOS that can be reconfigured to implement multiple machine learning algorithms such as SVM, DNN, and k-NN while showing 10x energy savings and 5x throughput enhancement.

Application-Specific ML accelerators using In-Memory Architectures

In this project, we focus on designing ML accelerators for some very popular ML algorithms for the edge. Designed the world's first dedicated IC for random forest algorithm using DIMA on 65 nm CMOS showing 6.8x improvement in energy-delay product.

We also designed a dedicated key-word spotting IC that achieves state-of-the-art decision latency of 40us with decision energy <0.5uJ/decision which translates to more than 24 times savings in the energy-delay product (EDP) of decisions over existing KWS ICs. This IC is ideal for products that have key-word triggered functionalities such as Amazon Alexa.

Variation-tolerant machine learning classifier via on-chip training

A major challenge in ICs that rely on analog computations such as DIMA, is its sensitivity to process variations. We studied the use of on-chip learning to achieve variation-tolerance in mixed-signal machine learning architectures. We designed a DIMA based classifier IC with an on-chip SGD based trainer on 65 nm CMOS which adapts to changing environment and process parameters. It demonstrates 2.4x energy reduction over previous ICs.

Machine learning acceleration using Beyond-CMOS devices

With CMOS scaling slowing down, new process technologies are needed to keep up with the demand for increased computing power. I worked on a broad spectrum of projects to address the challenges in realize ML accelerators using beyond CMOS devices: 1) I designed a NAND flash-based DIMA for large-scale machine learning acceleration showing an 8x reduction in energy consumption. 2) I worked an ML accelerator using MRAM devices that achieve 4.5x and 70x lower energy and delay than their CMOS counterparts. I worked on an algorithm that achieves high-accuracy writes for RRAM crossbar-based machine learning accelerators at 5x-to-10x lower cost than standard program-verify methods.
